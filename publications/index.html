  1. IEEE
    Advanced Hybrid Deep Learning Models for Solar Energy Holding Capacity Prediction in Bangladesh
    Aminul Islam, S. M. Obaydur Rahman, Akbar Hossin, and 1 more author
    In 2026 IEEE 2nd International Conference on Quantum Photonics, Artificial Intelligence, and Networking (QPAIN), Apr 2026
    Abs DOI Bib

    Bangladesh receives a significant amount of solar energy throughout the year, making it an ideal location to produce a substantial amount of solar energy. However, in many regions, the actual amount of solar energy produced is often lower than the amount installed due to unforeseen weather conditions and seasonal variations. This study introduces a hybrid stacking ensemble approach combining Random Forest, XGBoost, Gradient Boosting, and AdaBoost with an MLP meta-learner to predict solar energy holding capacity. The model achieves up to 95% accuracy and outperforms all individual base models, supporting efficient renewable energy planning and policy-making in Bangladesh.

    In silico investigations on curcuminoids from Curcuma longa as positive regulators of the Wnt/β-catenin signaling pathway in wound healing
    R. A. I. Reshad, S. Alam, H. B. Raihan, and 7 more authors
    Egyptian Journal of Medical Human Genetics, Jul 2021
    Abs DOI Bib HTML

    Curcuminoids from Curcuma longa were investigated via in silico molecular docking as positive regulators of key proteins in the Wnt/β-catenin signaling pathway. The study highlights the potential of curcuminoids in promoting wound healing through computational predictive modeling of genetic pathways.
